How to prepare pounded yam and vegetable soup

Ingredients

  • Yam
  • Water
  • Vegetable
  • Meat
  • Seasoning/dadawa
  • Mixed pepper
  • Palm oil or Groundout oil
  • Kabewa

Step one

Peel your yam wash and cut add into your pot with water
And allow to cook until soft
Then bring your mortar and pestle and start pounding your yam until smooth
You can be adding the hot water while pounding

Step two

For the soup Bring your pot on the fire add your oil onions
And fry then add your pepper mix cover and allow to fry

Step three

On the other side you have already boiled your meat and cut your vegetable

Step four

Then add your meat stock kabewa daddawa
And the remaining seasoning cover
And allow to cook when the water is almost dried up
Then you add yourvegetable cover
And allow to cook for 10minutes then your soup is ready

Step five

Serve with your pondo

How to make Jollof-rice

Ingredients

  • 4cups of long grain rice
  • 1 big onion
  • 1 canned tomato paste
  • 4 red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 2 scotch bonnet pepper ( Ata-rodo)
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • Seasoning cubes to taste
  • 1kg of meat or chicken
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 2 fingers of ginger
  • Ground nutmeg to taste
  • White pepper to taste

    • Step one

    Parboil your protein choice (meat or chicken) with the spices listed above.
    Remove from the water and set aside for later use.

    Step two

    Blend your red bell pepper, scotch bonnet, tomatoes, onions (three-quarter of your big onions), garlic and ginger
    And put to a boil till a thick paste is formed
    Where there’s little or no water left.

    Step three

    Fry your protein of choice in oil (I’m using olive oil) till it’s tender
    And golden brown then set aside.
    Meanwhile, wash and parboil your rice.

    Step four

    In a stainless/aluminium pot or saucepan
    Heat a little of the left-over oil from frying your protein
    Sauté the rest of your ominous till it’s fragrant and translucent.
    Add a teaspoon of thyme and curry, white pepper to taste and one finger
    A teaspoon of ground ginger alongside ground nutmeg.

    Step five

    Add your canned tomato paste and stir while frying.
    Fry till it has lost its acidic/sour taste.
    Then add the blended tomato-pepper mix and keep stirring while frying.
    At this point, get your meat/chicken water (stock) and add to the mix.
    Taste for seasoning and add your salt and/or seasoning cubes if needed.

    Step six

    Add your parboiled rice to your stew base and stir till all the grains are coated with the stew base.
    (You can choose to take out part of your stew base for later before adding your rice).
    Leave to simmer till all the water is absorbed.

    Step seven

    Check for doneness.
    Cover with a tight lid and leave to simmer for about 5 minutes then stir (let the steam do the rest of the work).
    Serve with your meat or chicken.
